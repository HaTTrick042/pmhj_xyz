Carnival Cruise: Smart Mirror
[Mock Proposal]

Problem Statement:
Given a class of students, design new technological solutions to the current Carnival Cruise experience and present those proposals with included marketing to personas, before/after comparisons, and possible gains associated with these proposals.

Plan:
After discussing where technological advancements would be most relevant in the Carnival Cruise experience we developed 2 proposals. A smart mirror and a smart band (developed by 2 outside team members). The smart mirror was designed to utilize a piece of common room space with a cool current spin.

Team:
2 members, Patrick Harper-Joles (Site/UI Design), Andrew Munsell (Technical Design)

Technology:
Sketch

Mildly Interesting:
This design had many interested during our in class presentation, and that weekend, another of our alma mater decided to make a prototype concept of a smart mirror. He got it working the following weekend during a 20 hour hacking session, just for fun!

    Design Notes

    • As a vacation with one's family is a special and valuable time of any year, technology should be best used to augment the experience but not pull one away from it, we tried to look to this value set for all aspects of this design
    • Informative but unobtrusive, simplified icons, small list of simplified tile based applications
    • The goal of this design is to ensure that a guest has access to all ship features and serves as a quick planner for deals, features, and connecting with other shipmates
  • Home View

    In order for a mirror to be useful in both its common physical and software properties, we decided early to use a monotone screen layout. The mirror could be transferred at any time with a quick swipe from it's mirror to smart mirror format. Additionally, a detailed and settings based view can be accessed by tapping any individual tile or note.

  • Reservations View

    Upon tapping the Itinerary tile, you can access the list of your upcoming reservations and the full list of the ships restaurant offerings. Any of these restaurant names can be tapped to go into it's detailed view. This allows for a central area to view all food places, their specials, and hours/location. This allows us to solve the problem of, what am I doing?

  • Detailed Restaurant View

    Tapping any of the restaurant names from the Reservations View will show you the detailed restaurant view including the menu, hours, and costs. A reservation can be made from this view by tapping the reservation icon in the top right of the view. This allows us to solve our second problem, what am I eating?

  • All Events View

    All events on the ship are presented, in order, with the ability to enter a Detailed Event View by tapping any events title. A quick reserve option for events that have a reservation list, is available to the left of the event title. We began to utilize color in this view to highlight differing information types. Color could be used in this design, but only for complex detail heavy interactions.

  • Detailed Event View

    Detailed event view allows for reservations, detailed event information, and also a list of people who have reserved the event. We wanted to ensure that there were social aspects built into the regular ship participation channels. During the family cruises, families can connect with other families and during singles cruises, singles can determine which events appear to best support their fun times. This solves our last question, who am I doing this with?

  • Event Attendees List

    This list, as part of the Detailed Event View, allows for you to see who is going to an event in detail and determine where the best party or family friendly time will be. As you meet new friends, you can elect to add them to your internal contacts list and send/receive invites from them.
